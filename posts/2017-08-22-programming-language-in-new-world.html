Human: A Manifesto for Programming Language for Humans

Abstract

Over the last 50 years, programming languages have been become increasingly human. It is far more easier to write programmes in the languages of today than it was 50 years ago. I argue that we are currently at the beginning of a wave of entirely new class of programming languages programming in which productivity will be orders of magnitude higher and quality will be orders of magnitude better. These programming languages will be very close to natural langues, and their compilers will be artifically intelligent. I discuss various charaterstics of this yet to be designed language, which I christen: Human.
